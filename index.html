Know what to look for

Signs your dryer vent may need attention

A slow dryer is not always an appliance problem. A qualified provider can assess the entire exhaust path and explain what they find.

Longer drying times

Loads need another cycle or take noticeably longer than they used to.

Excess heat

The appliance, clothes or laundry area feels unusually hot during a cycle.

Weak exterior airflow

The outside flap barely opens, or lint gathers around the vent outlet.

Burning or musty odour

Stop the dryer if you smell burning or suspect an immediate fire or electrical hazard.

Toronto-specific planning

The property and vent route shape the visit

A downtown condo with a long concealed run is different from a short side-wall vent in a Toronto house. The useful details are the laundry floor, appliance access, known outlet location, vent length or bends if known, and any building requirements.

Requests can cover Toronto neighbourhoods across Downtown, North York, Scarborough, Etobicoke, East York and York, subject to participating-provider availability.

Planning a condo visit? Start with property management and confirm who controls the in-suite and common portions of the exhaust route.

Details that improve a Toronto quote

  • House, townhouse, condo or multi-unit property
  • Basement, main-floor or upper-floor laundry
  • Stacked, gas or tightly enclosed appliance
  • Wall, balcony, soffit or roof outlet if known
  • Slow drying, excess heat, odour or routine maintenance
  • Parking, elevator or insurance requirements

Questions worth asking before booking

Price matters, but it does not tell you what is included. Use these prompts to compare like with like.

Inspection & scope

Will the full vent route and exterior termination be assessed? How are restrictions or disconnected sections identified?

Access & equipment

Does the quote cover moving the dryer, roof or balcony access, booster fans, long runs and additional bends?

Credentials

Ask the provider to confirm current insurance, relevant experience and any building-specific documentation.

Before-and-after evidence

Ask how results will be documented and what happens if damage or a vent repair is discovered.
