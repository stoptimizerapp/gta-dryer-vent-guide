Oakville homes are not one-size-fits-all

A main-floor laundry against an outside wall can present a very different route from an upper-floor laundry in a newer multi-level build. Homes around Bronte, Glen Abbey and River Oaks also vary in age, layout and exterior access.

Tell the provider where the dryer sits, where you believe the vent terminates and whether the appliance can be moved safely. For condo or townhome properties, include any property-management rules.

Details that can affect access

  • Upper-floor or basement laundry
  • Roof, soffit or high side-wall termination
  • Long run or several bends
  • Closet installation or stacked appliance
  • Parking and building access

Airflow or visual checks

Separate repair approval

Oakville maintenance notes

Pay attention to performance changes

If similar loads suddenly take longer, the dryer or room becomes hotter, or the exterior flap stops opening normally, the exhaust route may warrant assessment. These signs can have more than one cause, so avoid assuming cleaning alone will solve the problem.

Keep the outdoor outlet visible

Leaves, vegetation, snow and animal guards can affect an exterior termination. Health Canada recommends inspecting outside vents and keeping them clear. Do not reach into a vent or climb to an unsafe location.

Make the request specific

A specific request is easier to quote: “stacked dryer in a second-floor closet, exterior vent near the roofline” is more useful than “dryer is slow.”
