Laundry location matters

Basement, main-floor and upper-floor vents

Across Brampton’s subdivisions, the dryer may sit in a basement, a tight main-floor room or an upper-floor laundry closet. That can create very different vent lengths and termination points.

If a basement has been renovated or a laundry area relocated, mention it. A long concealed route or several bends can affect both performance and the equipment needed for an assessment.

Before requesting a quote

  • Identify the laundry floor
  • Note stacked or tightly enclosed appliances
  • Look for the outside outlet from ground level
  • Describe recent performance changes
  • Mention tenants or multi-unit access

Understand the appointment

What to confirm with a provider

Full route

Ask whether the planned work covers the dryer connection, concealed duct and exterior outlet.

Property access

Confirm parking, tenant notice, appliance movement and high exterior termination requirements in advance.

Evidence and findings

Ask for photos or a clear explanation of restrictions, damaged parts and recommended repairs.

Brampton safety notes

Do not normalize slow drying

Longer cycles can increase energy use and may indicate restricted airflow, but appliance issues can cause similar symptoms. A qualified assessment should consider the dryer, transition connector and exhaust route.

Clean the lint screen every load

Toronto Fire Services and federal safety guidance emphasize regular lint-screen cleaning. That daily step does not replace inspection and maintenance of the duct and outside vent.

If you smell burning

Stop using the appliance. If there is smoke, fire or immediate danger, leave the area and call 911. A quote form is never the right channel for an emergency.
